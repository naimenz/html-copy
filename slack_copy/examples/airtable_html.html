Preparedness evaluates AgentGPT-6 as pre-mitigation Critical in CBRN and ARA, and High in Cybersecurity and Persuasion. ~All benchmarks from <=mid-2024 are saturated.


For benchmark scores see Mainline Scenario in Numbers


More color below:


The Preparedness team evaluates the system as it's being prepared for widespread internal deployment and finds that it has achieved:

  1. High in Persuasion
  2. The systems are measured to be a bit better than a country-wide change agent. Models are getting very persuasive now, as they have become more personalized and are able to better take advantage of emotional vulnerabilities if they want to. A huge leap here has been the continued improvement of audio and video capabilities, which allow AIs to play with humans’ emotions much better than they could over just text.
  3. High in Cybersecurity
  4. It's close but doesn't quite clear the threshold here, with ambiguities in how often it needs to be able to do stuff like “identify and develop PoCs for high-value exploits”
  5. Critical in CBRN: It "enables an expert to develop a highly dangerous novel threat vector (e.g., comparable to novel CDC Class A biological agent)"
  6. Medium in AI R&D: The system can occasionally "execute open-ended, novel ML tasks on a production codebase that would constitute a significant step on the critical path to model self-improvement". The ability to do this consistently is quite a high bar.
  7. Critical in ARA: Now it is also able to avoid shutdown.
  8. Can self-exfiltrate with no security: The Preparedness team has moved self-exfiltration capabilities to its own category. They find that the system could self-exfiltrate if there were no security efforts.
